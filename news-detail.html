Stocks Churn With Earnings in Focus; Dow Slides

US stocks struggled to gain traction amid concern over the outlook for corporate earnings weighed on sentiment while investors assessed the path forward for central bank tightening.

The S&P 500 fell back below the 4,000 level. The Dow Jones Industrial Average underperformed, dropping 1%, with financials weighing on the gauge of blue chips. The tech-heavy Nasdaq 100 posted modest gains after a six-day rally, outperforming benchmarks amid a drop in yields.

Goldman Sachs Group Inc. slid the most in a year after reporting fourth-quarter net revenue below expectations, while Morgan Stanley rallied as its wealth-management business boosted revenue above forecasts. Pfizer Inc. retreated after Wells Fargo & Co. predicted earnings downgrades for the drugs company.

The S&P 500 was little changed as of 11:52 a.m. New York time

The Nasdaq 100 rose 0.1%

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 1%
